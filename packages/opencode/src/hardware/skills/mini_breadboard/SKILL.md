# Skill: Mini Breadboard (170 Tie-Points)

## Deep Intelligence: Engineering Wisdom
- **The Center Groove**: The middle gap is designed to fit standard DIP-package ICs (Integrated Circuits). Each side of the groove has 5 holes that are electrically connected horizontally.
- **Power Rails**: Unlike larger breadboards (400 or 830 points), mini breadboards usually DO NOT have dedicated vertical power/ground rails. You must manually bridge power to each section as needed.
- **Continuity Check**: If a circuit fails, always check if the component legs are deep enough. Cheap breadboards can have loose internal clips.
- **Adhesive Back**: Most mini breadboards come with a sticky foam back. This is useful for mounting on robot chassis or the back of an Arduino shield.

## Hardware Specs
- **Tie-points**: 170.
- **Rows**: 17.
- **Columns**: 10 (parallel sets of 5).
- **Pitch**: Standard 0.1" (2.54mm) - fits most through-hole components.

## Pro-Grade Prototyping Guide
- **Grouping**: Use different breadboards for different logic sections (e.g., one for sensors, one for power management).
- **Color Coding**: Use Red wires for VCC and Black/Blue for GND even on breadboards to avoid confusion.
- **Wire Gauge**: Use 22 AWG solid core wire or standard breadboard jumper wires. Don't force thick component legs (like large diodes) into the holes as it can deform the clips.
